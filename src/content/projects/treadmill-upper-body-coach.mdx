## The constraint that makes it work
Most running-form apps die on setup friction. On a treadmill, the phone is already propped up (often for Netflix), which gives you:
- repeatable camera placement
- a built-in user habit

That constraint makes an upper-body-first coach realistic.

## What it targets (upper-body cues)
From a front-facing treadmill view you can get useful signals without perfect full-body capture:
- vertical bounce (head/shoulder oscillation)
- trunk lean drift / slouching
- shoulder hiking / tension
- arm swing crossing midline
- asymmetry in arm swing
- forward head posture

## v1 pipeline
- person detect
- keypoints (nose/ears/shoulders/elbows/wrists/hips if visible)
- temporal smoothing
- “coach state machine” that only speaks when confident + persistent

## Product decisions
- **Setup gate**: verify torso/arms are in frame, lighting ok, low vibration; otherwise give one-line fix.
- **Separate pose from advice**: pose every frame, advice every 10–30s when consistent.
